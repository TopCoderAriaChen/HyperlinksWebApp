History of hyperlinks

The history of hyperlinks can be traced back to the 1960s. As computer technology and the internet have evolved, so has the application and use of hyperlinks in the presentation and organization of material. Below is an overview of the history of hyperlinks, as well as their key technologies and applications at different times.

The Origin of Hyperlinks
  • Concept proposal - The concept of hyperlinks was first proposed by Ted Nelson in 1965, who envisioned a global information space composed of hypertext in the "Xanadu" project.

Hyperlink restricted

Recent innovations such as Single Page Applications (SPAs), mobile apps, and social media are changing the way we use hyperlinks, which may, to some extent, undermine hyperlinks and the user autonomy they support. In SPAs, user interactions with the page no longer require the entire page to be reloaded, reducing the use of hyperlinks in the traditional sense. Mobile apps, through their built-in navigation and interface design, allow users to transition within the app rather than through hyperlinks. Social media platforms, with their closed content ecosystems, restrict the flow of external links, thus also affecting the traditional function of hyperlinks. These changes may limit users' ability to freely explore the internet, as they are more directed in consuming information along specific paths.
