History of hyperlinks

The history of hyperlinks can be traced back to the 1960s. As computer technology and the internet have evolved, so has the application and use of hyperlinks in the presentation and organization of material. Below is an overview of the history of hyperlinks, as well as their key technologies and applications at different times.

The Origin of Hyperlinks
  • Concept proposal - The concept of hyperlinks was first proposed by Ted Nelson in 1965, who envisioned a global information space composed of hypertext in the "Xanadu" project.

  • Early Implementations - Douglas Engelbart implemented an early form of hyperlinks in the "Augment Project" in the late 1960s, demonstrating hypertext links for the first time in his 1968 "Mother of All Demos."

The Development of Hyperlinks
  • HTML and the World Wide Web - Hyperlinks truly became widespread with the invention of the World Wide Web (WWW) and HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) by Tim Berners-Lee in 1991. In HTML, hyperlinks are implemented using the tag, marking the beginning of widespread use of hyperlinks on the internet.

    "The World's First Web Site". HISTORY. https://www.history.com/news/the-worlds-first-web-site

  • The Rise of Browsers - With the introduction of browsers like Netscape Navigator and Microsoft Internet Explorer, hyperlinks began to appear in richer forms, including image links and dynamic effects.
